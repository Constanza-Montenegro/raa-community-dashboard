Methodology Notes

Data collection

All data is self-reported by initiatives via a standardized Google Form, linked from the “Join with my initiative” button. Submissions enter the central registry and undergo a curation process before being released to the dashboard.

Achieved vs Projected

Achieved values reflect outcomes already delivered by initiatives between 2015 and 2025. Projected values represent the commitments and goals set by initiatives for the 2026–2030 period.

Indicators tracked

Quantitative (split into Achieved & Projected): hectares under restoration and conservation, inland waters under restoration and conservation, people benefited, and financial resources mobilized.

Categorical: type of actor, geographic scope, region, priority area, priority ecosystem, beneficiary, critical enabler, Rio Convention synergies, and alignment with Land & Soil Breakthroughs.

Update frequency

New submissions are not published instantly. Each entry passes through a curation review and, once approved, is released to the dashboard. From that point onwards, updates are reflected within minutes.

Scope & limitations

The snapshot aggregates only initiatives that have joined the RAA Community. No ranking, scoring, or independent verification is applied to the reported figures.

Land & Soil Breakthroughs

Initiatives flagged as engaged with Land & Soil Breakthroughs are those formally aligned with this partnership at the time of reporting.

Financial figures

All financial commitments and mobilizations are aggregated in this snapshot regardless of public disclosure preferences. The disclosure flag set by each initiative governs visibility on individual profile cards only, not the community-level totals shown here.

Multi-value categories

Initiatives may select multiple values per category (priority areas, beneficiaries, enablers, etc.). Bar charts count each occurrence, so totals across a category can exceed the number of initiatives.

Restoration vs Conservation

Restoration refers to active interventions to recover degraded land or ecosystems — such as replanting, soil rehabilitation, or rewetting.

Conservation refers to protecting land that is still in good condition from further degradation. Both are reported separately in hectares and inland waters.

By Critical Enabler

Critical Enablers are the cross-cutting mechanisms the RAA uses to accelerate action:

💰 Finance — Mobilize investment and de-risk capital through instruments such as carbon markets, KPI-linked bonds and debt-for-nature swaps.

🏛️ Governance — Strengthen inclusive, rights-based decision-making and equitable resource allocation, including for historically excluded groups.

🔬 Science, Technology & Innovation — Bridge mainstream science with indigenous and local knowledge to drive data-informed, locally-led restoration.

By Rio Synergies

The three Rio Conventions were established at the 1992 Earth Summit:

🌱 UNCCD — UN Convention to Combat Desertification
🌿 CBD — Convention on Biological Diversity
🌡️ UNFCCC — UN Framework Convention on Climate Change

Initiatives may declare alignment with one or more of these frameworks beyond their core land mandate.

Land & Soil Breakthroughs

A Breakthrough is a shared, time-bound goal that drives a decisive system shift — from fragmented efforts to coordinated, large-scale action.

Rather than creating new initiatives, Breakthroughs provide a unifying framework that connects existing efforts, aligns actors around science-based targets, and makes collective progress visible.

The Land & Soil Breakthrough Targets are developed under the RAA in connection with the UNCCD space, inspired by the Sharm El-Sheikh Adaptation Agenda.
